## What the brand name means

Pikorua is a Māori double-twist symbol representing **eternal trust, lifelong connections, and two paths joining**. This is not decoration — it is the brand's philosophical core. Every piece of copy should feel like it could have been written by someone who understands what it means to earn trust over a lifetime, not close a deal in an afternoon.

---

## Target Buyer (one sentence)

A discerning individual — HNI or NRI, 35–60, net worth ₹5 Cr+ — who is not buying a property but choosing the context in which their life or legacy will unfold, and who will only work with an advisor they trust completely.

---

## Emotion we want the buyer to feel

**Quiet confidence.** The sense that they've already arrived — that this property, this advisor, this moment was curated for someone exactly like them. Not excitement. Not urgency. Certainty.

- affordable
- deal
- cheap / cheapest
- limited time offer / limited time only
- hurry / act fast / act now
- don't miss out / FOMO
- best price / lowest price
- book now
- discount / discounted
- offer valid till
- starting from [low anchor price]
- unbeatable
- guaranteed returns [CONFIRM — likely off-brand]

## Preferred CTA style

**Yes:**
- "Schedule a private viewing"
- "Request a curated shortlist"
- "Speak with Jitendra directly"
- "Explore by appointment"
- "Let's find yours"

**No:**
- "Contact us today"
- "Call now"
- "Fill this form"
- "Buy now"
- "Get a free quote"

The CTA should feel like an invitation, not a conversion mechanism.

## Tone notes

**Register:** The tone of a trusted wealth manager writing to a long-standing client. Warm but not familiar. Confident but not boastful. Specific but not exhaustive.

**Sentence structure:** Prefer shorter sentences. No run-ons. Luxury copy breathes — it does not stack clauses.

**Specificity is everything:** "A 3BHK sea-view apartment on Sindhu Bhavan Road" beats "a luxury apartment in Ahmedabad" every time. Generic copy is the enemy.

**Restraint:** If a line feels like it's trying too hard, it is. Cut it. Pikorua earns attention through understatement, not volume.

**NRI angle:** When writing for NRI buyers, the emotional hook is reconnection, legacy, and belonging — not financial return alone. "Your India address" is a more powerful frame than "high ROI property."

**Platform-specific tone adjustments:**
- Meta Ads: Short, evocative, visual-first. The copy should work alongside a beautiful image, not compete with it.
- Google Ads: More functional, keyword-aware, but still no urgency language.
- WhatsApp: Warm, personal, advisor-to-client. Write as if Jitendra is sending it personally.
- Email: The most considered format. Assume the reader has 90 seconds and a high attention threshold.
